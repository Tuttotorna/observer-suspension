# Causality Cases v0

This document contains the first focused case set for causality under Protocol O1.

The purpose is not to deny causality.

The purpose is to test whether ordinary human causal language depends on observer-centered narrative compression, and whether suspending that compression reveals a more general structure.

---

## Why causality is selected first

Human cognition strongly prefers causal formulations that are:

- linear
- directional
- readable
- source-centered
- narratively compact

This preference is useful for local reasoning and rapid coordination.

But it may also hide:

- distributed constraint
- conditional dependency
- recursive interaction
- multi-factor structure
- system-level configuration effects

Protocol O1 is applied here to test whether observer suspension yields structural gain in causal description.

---

## Case 1 - "A causes B"

### 1. Standard human formulation
`A causes B.`

### 2. Implicit observer assumption
- The observer compresses the system into a single readable directional arrow.
- The human preference for one source and one effect is treated as structurally primary.
- Explanation is identified with narratable direction.

### 3. Distortion introduced
- Multi-factor dependency is reduced to a single-line sequence.
- Conditions of activation disappear.
- Constraint structure is hidden behind a story-like arrow.

### 4. De-centered reformulation
Under a given configuration, a stable dependency between A and B appears.  
The apparent direction may be a local compression of a richer dependency structure.

### 5. Emerging structure
- Dependency replaces automatic narrative causation.
- Conditions become explicit.
- Direction is no longer primitive by default.

### O1 outcome
**Outcome C - strong structural gain**

## Case 2 - "The event happened because of X"

### 1. Standard human formulation
`The event happened because of X.`

### 2. Implicit observer assumption
- The observer privileges one legible source as explanatory center.
- A complex event is treated as if it had one narratively sufficient origin.
- Readability is mistaken for completeness.

### 3. Distortion introduced
- Supporting conditions are erased.
- Alternative interacting factors are hidden.
- The event is retrofitted into a compact explanatory story.

### 4. De-centered reformulation
Event E appears under a configuration in which X is one active dependency among other enabling conditions, thresholds, and interactions.

### 5. Emerging structure
- X becomes one contributor, not automatically the sole cause.
- Event structure becomes configurational.
- Explanation shifts from source-labeling to dependency mapping.

### O1 outcome
**Outcome C - strong structural gain**

## Case 3 - "The system failed because component Y broke"

### 1. Standard human formulation
`The system failed because component Y broke.`

### 2. Implicit observer assumption
- Failure is compressed into a single readable fault origin.
- The observer seeks one break-point as explanatory anchor.
- Sequential fault narration is treated as sufficient structural account.

### 3. Distortion introduced
- System architecture, redundancy, latent fragility, and preconditions are hidden.
- Failure propagation is collapsed into one local event.
- Structural vulnerability is mistaken for isolated local cause.

### 4. De-centered reformulation
System failure occurred under a configuration in which component Y entered a non-functional state within a broader dependency structure that did not absorb or redistribute the perturbation.

### 5. Emerging structure
- Failure becomes a property of the whole dependency network, not only of Y.
- Local breakage and global fragility are separated.
- Architecture becomes part of the explanation.

### O1 outcome
**Outcome C - strong structural gain**

## Case 4 - "One decision changed everything"

### 1. Standard human formulation
`One decision changed everything.`

### 2. Implicit observer assumption
- The observer privileges dramatic discrete moments.
- Human narrative preference selects one visible pivot as total explanatory center.
- Global change is compressed into one memorable event.

### 3. Distortion introduced
- Gradual conditions, accumulated tensions, and path dependence disappear.
- The phrase converts a distributed transition into a turning-point myth.
- "Everything" becomes rhetorically inflated.

### 4. De-centered reformulation
A decision acted as a high-impact transition within an already prepared dependency landscape, amplifying and redirecting multiple ongoing processes.

### 5. Emerging structure
- The decision becomes a transition operator, not a magical source.
- Path dependence and preconfiguration reappear.
- Large-scale change is distributed across prior structure and later propagation.

### O1 outcome
**Outcome C - strong structural gain**

## Case 5 - "The market crashed because investors lost confidence"

### 1. Standard human formulation
`The market crashed because investors lost confidence.`

### 2. Implicit observer assumption
- The observer reduces a complex system to a psychologically readable cause.
- A narrative centered on human emotion is treated as sufficient explanation.
- Macro-dynamics are compressed into one interpretive storyline.

### 3. Distortion introduced
- Structural, institutional, liquidity, feedback, and timing effects are hidden.
- "Lost confidence" becomes a narrative substitute for multi-level interaction.
- Human legibility overrides system complexity.

### 4. De-centered reformulation
Market decline emerged under a configuration involving investor behavior shifts together with broader structural conditions, feedback loops, and system sensitivities; "loss of confidence" names only one compressed description of part of the transition.

### 5. Emerging structure
- Psychological explanation loses monopoly.
- Feedback and sensitivity become explicit.
- Causality becomes distributed and configuration-dependent.

### O1 outcome
**Outcome C - strong structural gain**

## Case 6 - "Fear causes violence"

### 1. Standard human formulation
`Fear causes violence.`

### 2. Implicit observer assumption
- The observer turns a broad pattern into a direct human-readable arrow.
- A complex behavioral field is reduced to a simple emotional source.
- Population variation and mediating factors are ignored.

### 3. Distortion introduced
- Context, thresholds, norms, incentives, histories, and situational constraints disappear.
- A probabilistic relation is recast as deterministic cause.
- The statement becomes stronger than the structure supports.

### 4. De-centered reformulation
Fear may participate in a dependency structure that increases the probability of violent behavior under specific contextual, social, and regulatory conditions.

### 5. Emerging structure
- Causality becomes conditional and probabilistic.
- Mediating structure becomes necessary.
- Emotional state alone stops functioning as total explanation.

### O1 outcome
**Outcome B/C - moderate to strong structural gain**

## Case 7 - "Technology makes people lonely"

### 1. Standard human formulation
`Technology makes people lonely.`

### 2. Implicit observer assumption
- The observer gives one broad object-category a total causal role.
- Complex social transformation is compressed into a single modern villain.
- Human narrative prefers one readable external driver.

### 3. Distortion introduced
- Technology is treated as a homogeneous agent.
- Social patterns, usage modes, institutions, incentives, and selection effects are hidden.
- "Lonely" remains vague while causality is overstated.

### 4. De-centered reformulation
Some technological configurations, usage patterns, and institutional environments may participate in dependency structures associated with increased social isolation under specific conditions.

### 5. Emerging structure
- Technology becomes heterogeneous.
- Conditions and mediators re-enter the frame.
- The statement shifts from slogan-causality to structured dependency.

### O1 outcome
**Outcome C - strong structural gain**

## Case 8 - "The drug cured the patient"

### 1. Standard human formulation
`The drug cured the patient.`

### 2. Implicit observer assumption
- The observer privileges one intervention as principal cause of the outcome.
- Recovery is narratively attributed to a single visible treatment.
- The patient-system is simplified into intervention -> result.

### 3. Distortion introduced
- Baseline variation, spontaneous recovery, dosage, timing, interaction effects, and system condition may be hidden.
- The outcome is attached too strongly to one visible factor.
- Clinical complexity is compressed into treatment success story.

### 4. De-centered reformulation
Patient recovery occurred under a treatment configuration in which the drug may have played an active role together with baseline condition, timing, dosage, interactions, and other contributing factors.

### 5. Emerging structure
- Cure becomes outcome within a treatment-state system.
- Single-factor attribution weakens.
- Clinical dependency structure becomes more visible.

### O1 outcome
**Outcome B/C - moderate to strong structural gain**

## Preliminary causal pattern

Across these first cases, ordinary causal language repeatedly shows the same observer-centered compression:

1. one readable source is privileged
2. one directional story is preferred
3. conditions are erased
4. distributed dependency is hidden
5. explanation is mistaken for narratability

This does not prove that direction never exists.

It only shows that human causal description often starts by privileging a narrative center before the structure has been fully mapped.

---

## First structural replacement

A first replacement for naive causal language may be:

### Human default
`A causes B`

### O1 de-centered form
`B emerges under a configuration in which A participates as one active dependency under given conditions.`

This replacement is heavier in wording, but structurally richer.

It suspends the privileged observer's need for a simple readable arrow and reopens the space of:

- condition
- configuration
- mediation
- feedback
- distributed structure

---

## What would count as progress from here

Further work on causality should only continue if repeated O1 applications reveal reusable patterns such as:

- condition-dependent dependency
- multi-source emergence
- feedback-sensitive propagation
- configuration-level failure or transition
- non-primitive directionality
- narrative compression as local summary rather than primary structure

If these patterns recur strongly, then a stronger formal layer for de-centered causality becomes justified.

If not, this document should remain a useful but limited exploratory archive.
