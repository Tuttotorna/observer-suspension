# observer-suspension Protocol Scope

## Purpose

observer-suspension is a structural protocol for exposing hidden observer privilege in descriptions.

It is designed to test whether a statement can be reformulated with less frame dependence while preserving the phenomenon being described.

## Boundary

```text
measurement != inference != decision
observer reduction != semantic judgment
decentering != truth proof
structural gain != final truth
decision remains external
```

## What is in scope

The protocol may analyze:

- hidden observer assumptions
- frame dependence
- perspective compression
- relational structure
- narrative compression
- observer-centered wording
- decentered reformulation quality
- structural gain
- reconstruction vs evaporation

## What is out of scope

observer-suspension does not provide:

- philosophy
- worldview
- metaphysical proof
- truth oracle
- semantic judgment
- final decision
- complete theory of reality
- complete theory of time
- complete theory of causality

## Correct reading

```text
O1 is a structural protocol.
O1 exposes observer privilege.
O1 tests reformulation quality.
O1 can fail.
Failure is evidence about the limit of decentering.
```
