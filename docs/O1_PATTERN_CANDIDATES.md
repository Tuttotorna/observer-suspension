# O1 Pattern Candidates

This document extracts the first recurring structural patterns emerging from Protocol O1.

The purpose is not to declare a final formal system.

The purpose is to identify whether repeated suspension of the privileged observer produces reusable transformations of description.

If such patterns recur across domains, Protocol O1 may justify a stronger formal layer.

If not, O1 should remain a local exploratory method.

---

## Why this document matters

The repository now contains:

- the general framework
- Protocol O1
- first reference cases
- focused case sets for causality
- focused case sets for time

At this stage, the correct next move is not to add endless examples.

The correct next move is to ask:

**Are the same structural shifts happening again and again?**

If yes, these recurring shifts may form the first real grammar of observer suspension.

---

## Pattern 1 - Absolute -> Relational

### Human default
A property is treated as intrinsic, absolute, or self-standing.

Examples:
- the object is still
- the sun rises
- the past is fixed

### O1 shift
The apparently absolute property is reformulated as relational, frame-dependent, or accessibility-dependent.

### Structural gain
- intrinsic language weakens
- frame and relation become explicit
- description becomes less observer-naive

### Candidate formula
`absolute property -> relational configuration`

### Notes
This is one of the strongest and most recurrent O1 effects.

---

## Pattern 2 - Phenomenology -> Structure

### Human default
A lived experience is treated as primitive world-structure.

Examples:
- time flows
- the present is now
- duration passes

### O1 shift
Phenomenological immediacy is reformulated as ordered difference, access index, metric separation, or transition relation.

### Structural gain
- local experience loses ontological privilege
- structural description begins below lived metaphor
- system-organization becomes primary

### Candidate formula
`lived experience -> configuration / ordering / transition structure`

### Notes
This pattern is especially strong in temporal cases.

---

## Pattern 3 - Narrative Arrow -> Conditional Dependency

### Human default
A readable directional story is treated as explanation.

Examples:
- A causes B
- the event happened because of X
- the market crashed because investors lost confidence

### O1 shift
The arrow is reformulated as conditional dependency inside a broader configuration.

### Structural gain
- conditions reappear
- multi-factor participation becomes visible
- explanation is separated from narratability

### Candidate formula
`narrative cause -> conditioned dependency in configuration`

### Notes
This is the strongest causal pattern observed so far.

---

## Pattern 4 - Single Center -> Local Index

### Human default
A privileged center is assumed:
- the observer
- the present
- the local point of view
- the visible frame

### O1 shift
The privileged center is reformulated as a local index, not as universal ontological anchor.

### Structural gain
- local access is preserved without being universalized
- descriptive position becomes explicit
- center loses metaphysical privilege

### Candidate formula
`privileged center -> local descriptive index`

### Notes
This pattern may be one of the deepest in the entire framework.

---

## Pattern 5 - Container Language -> Transition Language

### Human default
Processes are described as occurring "in" or "through" vaguely treated containers.

Examples:
- things unfold over time
- duration passes between changes
- change happens over time

### O1 shift
The container metaphor is replaced by transition structure, ordering, configuration-change, or dependency relations.

### Structural gain
- vague backdrop language weakens
- process becomes analyzable through relations between states
- explanatory force shifts from metaphor to structure

### Candidate formula
`container metaphor -> state-transition organization`

### Notes
This pattern appears especially promising in time-related cases.

---

## Pattern 6 - Readability -> Compression

### Human default
If a statement is readable, compact, and intuitive, it is treated as a good structural account.

Examples:
- one decision changed everything
- fear causes violence
- technology makes people lonely

### O1 shift
Readable formulation is treated as compression, not as final structure.

### Structural gain
- explanation is no longer equated with simplicity
- slogan-like formulations are unpacked
- compression itself becomes an object of analysis

### Candidate formula
`readable explanation -> local compression of richer structure`

### Notes
This pattern may become central if the framework expands beyond time and causality.

---

## Pattern 7 - Primitive Object -> Stabilized Configuration

### Human default
Objects are treated as simply given.

Examples:
- I see a tree
- the object is there
- the thing exists as itself

### O1 shift
Objecthood is reformulated as stabilized configuration, segmentation result, or persistence-pattern.

### Structural gain
- primitive givenness weakens
- objecthood becomes emergent or relational
- perception and classification re-enter the picture

### Candidate formula
`given object -> stabilized configuration`

### Notes
This pattern is promising but not yet developed enough in the repository.

---

## Pattern 8 - Sequence -> Partial Order

### Human default
Events are assumed to occur in one readable line.

Examples:
- events happen in sequence
- first this, then that
- one step leads to the next

### O1 shift
Sequential description is weakened into partial order, branching order, local ordering, or dependency ordering.

### Structural gain
- parallelism becomes thinkable
- branching structures reappear
- serial cognition stops dictating ontology

### Candidate formula
`linear sequence -> partial or local ordering`

### Notes
This pattern may be crucial for time and possibly for causality.

## Preliminary pattern table

| Pattern | Human default | O1 shift |
|---|---|---|
| Absolute -> Relational | intrinsic property | frame/configuration relation |

| Narrative Arrow -> Conditional Dependency | readable cause | configured dependency |
| Single Center -> Local Index | privileged center | indexed local position |
| Container Language -> Transition Language | vague backdrop | state-transition structure |

## What would count as real progress

These pattern candidates become meaningful only if they satisfy at least one of the following:

1. they recur across multiple domains
2. they support cleaner reformulations than ordinary language
3. they begin to compose into a reusable method
4. they generate genuinely new descriptive leverage
5. they support stronger formalization later

If they remain isolated observations, then they are not yet a framework.

---

## Current strongest candidates

At the present stage, the strongest recurring patterns appear to be:

1. **Narrative Arrow -> Conditional Dependency**
2. **Phenomenology -> Structure**
3. **Single Center -> Local Index**
4. **Absolute -> Relational**

These four seem to form the first serious candidate core of O1.

---

## Provisional conclusion

Protocol O1 does not yet yield a full formal grammar.

But it does appear to produce recurring structural shifts rather than isolated paraphrases.

This is the first sign that observer suspension may be more than a conceptual exercise.

If these patterns continue to recur under more difficult applications, then O1 may justify a stronger formal and comparative layer.
