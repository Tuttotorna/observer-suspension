# First Cases - Protocol O1

This document contains the first reference cases analyzed under Protocol O1.

The purpose of these cases is not to prove a theory, but to test whether suspending the privileged human observer yields non-trivial structural clarification.

---

## Case 1 - "The sun rises"

### 1. Standard human formulation
`The sun rises.`

### 2. Implicit observer assumption
- A local terrestrial observer is treated as the natural descriptive center.
- The local horizon is silently privileged.
- A relational configuration is expressed as if it were an absolute event.

### 3. Distortion introduced
- A geometric relation becomes an event attributed to the Sun itself.
- The local observer frame is mistaken for world structure.
- Relational visibility is compressed into narrative occurrence.

### 4. De-centered reformulation
The relative configuration between Earth, local horizon, and luminous source changes so that the source becomes visible above the local horizon.

### 5. Emerging structure
- "Rising" is not an intrinsic property of the Sun.
- The phenomenon is frame-relative.
- The event is a local visibility effect within a relational system.

### O1 outcome
**Outcome B - moderate structural clarification**

## Case 2 - "The object is still"

### 1. Standard human formulation
`The object is still.`

### 2. Implicit observer assumption
- A tacit frame of reference is treated as neutral.
- Stillness is treated as an intrinsic property of the object.

### 3. Distortion introduced
- Frame-relative stability is converted into absolute rest.
- The descriptive dependence on the selected frame is hidden.

### 4. De-centered reformulation
Within the selected frame, the relative positional variation of the object is negligible.

### 5. Emerging structure
- Stillness is relational, not absolute.
- The frame becomes explicit.
- The statement shifts from substance-language to relation-language.

### O1 outcome
**Outcome B - moderate structural clarification**

---

## Case 3 - "Time flows"

### 1. Standard human formulation
`Time flows.`

### 2. Implicit observer assumption
- Human lived succession is projected into the structure of reality.
- Phenomenological experience is treated as ontological primitive.

### 3. Distortion introduced
- A human experience of change is reified into a flowing entity.
- Sequential consciousness is mistaken for fundamental temporal structure.

### 4. De-centered reformulation
Ordered differences between system configurations are observed; "flow" is a local phenomenological description rather than a necessary primitive of the system.

### 5. Emerging structure
- Temporal flow is no longer primary.
- State-ordering becomes more basic than lived succession.
- Alternative temporal formalisms become thinkable.

### O1 outcome
**Outcome C - strong structural gain**

---

## Case 4 - "I see a tree"

### 1. Standard human formulation
`I see a tree.`

### 2. Implicit observer assumption
- A stable self is treated as primitive.
- A ready-made external object is treated as given.
- Perception is treated as passive access.

### 3. Distortion introduced
- The object appears to pre-exist its stabilization in perception.
- The active role of segmentation and classification is hidden.
- The observer/object split is treated as immediate and fundamental.

### 4. De-centered reformulation
A perceptual system stabilizes a portion of the sensory field into a configuration classified as a tree.

### 5. Emerging structure
- Objecthood appears as stabilized structure.
- Perception becomes an active process of configuration.
- Observer and observed are no longer primitively separated in the naive sense.

### O1 outcome
**Outcome C - strong structural gain**

---

## Case 5 - "A causes B"

### 1. Standard human formulation
`A causes B.`

### 2. Implicit observer assumption
- A complex system is compressed into a readable directional story.
- The observer privileges linear narrative dependence over distributed structure.

### 3. Distortion introduced
- Multi-dependency becomes single-arrow causation.
- Conditions and constraints are hidden behind sequence.
- Explanation is confused with narrative readability.

### 4. De-centered reformulation
A stable dependency between A and B appears under given conditions; the directional narrative may be a local compression of a richer structural configuration.

### 5. Emerging structure
- Causality becomes conditional dependency rather than immediate linear story.
- Constraint structure replaces default narrative arrow.
- Richer formalizations become possible.

### O1 outcome
**Outcome C - strong structural gain**

## Preliminary pattern

Across these first cases, Protocol O1 already shows three recurring effects:

1. It converts apparently absolute properties into relational structures.
2. It separates human phenomenology from system-level description.
3. It reduces narrative compression and exposes conditional dependency.

These outcomes do not yet prove a general framework.

But they are sufficient to justify deeper work on the two main priority directions:

- time without a privileged observer
- causality without a privileged observer
