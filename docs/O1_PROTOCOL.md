# O1 Protocol - Observer Suspension v0

## Status

Frozen minimal protocol.

O1 is the first operational protocol of Observer Suspension.

Its function is narrow and explicit:
identify observer-centered assumptions inside a statement and produce a reformulation with lower dependence on a privileged observer frame.

O1 does not explain reality.
O1 does not decide truth.
O1 does not produce ontology.

O1 only performs structural decomposition of observer-loaded statements.

---

## Core purpose

Human descriptions often compress hidden assumptions into apparently simple statements.

Typical hidden assumptions include:
- stable privileged observer
- fixed frame of reference
- object permanence treated as absolute
- linear time treated as intrinsic flow
- causal order narrated as if directly given
- perception described as if it were neutral access

O1 makes these assumptions visible and reduces their dominance in the final formulation.

---

## Input

A natural-language statement containing possible observer-centered compression.

Example input:
- "The sun rises"
- "The object is still"
- "Time passes"
- "I see a tree"
- "A causes B"

---

## Output schema

O1 returns exactly five fields.

### 1. Standard formulation
The original statement, unchanged.

### 2. Hidden observer assumption
The implicit observer-centered assumption required for the standard formulation to appear natural.

### 3. Structural distortion
What the assumption compresses, hides, or falsely stabilizes.

### 4. Decentered reformulation
A reformulation that reduces dependence on the privileged observer frame.

### 5. Emergent structure
The structural relation that becomes visible after decentering.

---

## Canonical field definitions

### Standard formulation
The ordinary statement as commonly expressed.

Constraint:
must preserve the original wording as input record.

### Hidden observer assumption
The minimal implicit condition that makes the statement appear straightforward.

Constraint:
state the assumption directly, not rhetorically.

Good:
"assumes an Earth-bound observer frame"

Bad:
"humans usually think this way"

### Structural distortion
The compression or misplacement introduced by the hidden assumption.

Constraint:
describe the distortion structurally, not psychologically.

Good:
"relative motion is collapsed into absolute motion"

Bad:
"this is misleading because humans are limited"

### Decentered reformulation
A transformed statement with lower dependence on privileged viewpoint.

Constraint:
must preserve informational intent while reducing observer privilege.

Good:
"the Earth's rotation changes the sun's apparent position relative to the observer"

Bad:
"everything is relative"

### Emergent structure
The relation that becomes explicit after decentering.

Constraint:
must identify relation, dependency, or frame structure.

Good:
"apparent solar motion depends on observer frame and planetary rotation"

Bad:
"reality is more complex"

---

## Operational rule

O1 should apply the weakest sufficient transformation.

Meaning:
do not rewrite more than necessary.
The goal is not abstraction for its own sake.
The goal is reduction of observer privilege.

If a small decentering is enough, stop there.

---

## Invariants

O1 must preserve:
- original informational target
- referential continuity
- explicit relation to the original statement

O1 must reduce:
- hidden observer privilege
- false absolutization
- narrative compression of relations

---

## What O1 is not

O1 is not:
- a semantic parser
- a truth verifier
- a scientific theory of everything
- a linguistic style corrector
- a metaphysical system

It is only a minimal structural protocol.

---

## Minimal procedure

For each input statement:

1. record the original sentence
2. detect the hidden observer assumption
3. identify the structural distortion
4. rewrite the sentence with reduced observer privilege
5. state the structure revealed by the rewrite

This defines one O1 record.

---

## Acceptance criteria for an O1 record

A valid O1 record must satisfy all of the following:

1. the hidden assumption is explicit
2. the distortion is structural, not rhetorical
3. the reformulation reduces privileged observer dependence
4. the reformulation still refers to the same target
5. the emergent structure is clearer than in the original

If one of these fails, the record is weak.

---

## Rejection conditions

Reject an O1 output if:
- it adds metaphysical claims
- it becomes more vague than the input
- it changes the target of the original sentence
- it replaces one hidden privilege with another
- it turns a concrete sentence into empty abstraction

---

## Example

### Standard formulation
"The sun rises"

### Hidden observer assumption
Assumes a stable Earth-bound observer frame treated as primary.

### Structural distortion
Relative apparent motion is compressed into an absolute event attributed to the sun.

### Decentered reformulation
"The sun's apparent position changes relative to an Earth-bound observer due to Earth's rotation."

### Emergent structure
Observed solar motion depends on observer frame and planetary rotation, not on an intrinsic rising event.

---

## Boundary

O1 is a decomposition protocol.
It is useful only if its outputs can later be compared, tested, or integrated into stronger structural analysis.

Without comparison or evaluation, O1 remains only a conceptual exercise.
